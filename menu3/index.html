In this lab, you will study some aspects of the motion shown the video above. In particular, we are interested in whether angular momentum and rotational kinetic energy are conserved through the transition from spinning and rolling to just rolling.

Theory

Fig.1 - From Jackson et al., Am. J. Phys., Feb. 2019

When a cylindrical object is spun as shown in the video, it actually also rolls without slipping on one edge. The figure above depicts a side view of this situation. Its motion has a lot of similarities to the precessing top discussed in lecture so please bear that in mind.

The important parameters are:

  • R R R: radius of cylinder

  • ℓ \ell ℓ: half of the length of the cylinder

  • ℓ~=ℓ/R \tilde{\ell} = \ell/R ℓ~=ℓ/R: equal to length of the cylinder divided by 2RRR.

  • Ω \Omega Ω: rate of rotation (angular velocity) about zzz-axis.

  • ψ˙ \dot{\psi} ψ˙​: rate of rotation about the cylinder's main principal axis.

  • e^z \hat{e}_z e^z​: Unit vector in the zzz direction. Same as k^\hat{k}k^.

  • e^1 \hat{e}_1 e^1​: Unit vector in the plane of the cylinder's perpendicular principal axis.

  • e^3 \hat{e}_3 e^3​: Unit vector in the direction of the cylinder's main principal axis (along its length).

  • rϕ r_\phi rϕ​: The part of the cylinder half-length ℓ\ellℓ that is parallel to the ground.

  • θ \theta θ: The angle that the cylinder makes with the vertical (measures how tipped over it is).

Since the edge of the cylinder traces out a circle of radius rϕr_\phirϕ​ on the ground while rolling about about its axis, we get

rϕΩ=Rψ˙ r_\phi\Omega = R\dot{\psi} rϕ​Ω=Rψ˙​

Now, we need to determine rϕ r_\phi rϕ​. Look at the diagram and try to determine what rϕ r_\phi rϕ​ would be in terms of R,ℓ R,\ell R,ℓ, and θ \theta θ.

rϕ=ℓsin⁡θ−Rcos⁡θ r_\phi = \ell\sin\theta - R\cos\theta rϕ​=ℓsinθ−Rcosθ
